FAQ

What is IoT?

IoT stands for Internet of Things. It refers to the network of physical objects or "things" embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies for the purpose of connecting and exchanging data with other devices and systems over the internet.

How does IoT work?

IoT devices collect data from their surroundings using sensors, process that data using embedded software, and then transmit the processed data over the internet to other connected devices or systems for further analysis or action.

What are some examples of IoT devices?

Examples of IoT devices include smart thermostats, wearable fitness trackers, connected home security systems, smart appliances (such as refrigerators and washing machines), and industrial sensors used in manufacturing and logistics.

What are the benefits of IoT?

Some of the benefits of IoT include improved efficiency and productivity, real-time monitoring and control of systems, enhanced decision-making through data analytics, cost savings, and the development of innovative products and services.
